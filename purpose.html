Purpose of Urinary Catheterization

There are two types of urethral catheterization: intermittent and indwelling.

Intermittent catheterization(single-lumen catheter) is used for:


1. Immediate relief of urinary retention
2. Long-term management of incompetent bladder
3. Obtaining a sterile urine specimen
4. Assessing residual urine in the bladder after voiding (if a bladder scanner is not available)



Indwelling catheterization (double- or triple-lumen catheter) is used for:



1. Promoting urinary elimination
2. Measuring accurate urine output
3. Preventing skin breakdown
4. Facilitating wound management
5. Allowing surgical repair of urethra, bladder, or surrounding structures
6. Instilling irrigation fluids or medications
7. Assessing abdominal/pelvic pain
8. Investigating conditions of the genitourinary system
